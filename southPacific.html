    Australia

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Australians are typically straightforward and direct business professionals. Therefore, expect little or no small talk.
    • Keep in mind that business meetings may last longer than expected. Patience is important and appreciated.

    Greetings

    • Shake hands with everyone present upon meeting and before leaving.
    • Allow women to offer their hands first.
    • Women generally do not shake hands with other women - only with other men.

    Social Conditions

    • Females may experience unwanted attention from men.
    • If you are the focus of unwanted attention, simply ignore the comments. It is probably not meant as an insult.

    Attire

    • Clothing for Australia is influenced by the region. It would be appropriate for female travelers to wear clothes made from moleskin and drill cotton, which are typically developed for practical wear.

    New Zealand

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • People of New Zealand have managed to create a relaxed but professional atmosphere that maintains equal rights.

    Greetings

    • New Zealanders are friendly people and greetings are often informal. However, people in New Zealand are typically private people, so try to avoid asking private information.
    • Handshakes, smiles and direct eye contact are part of greetings in New Zealand.

    Social Conditions

    • Smoking is illegal in New Zealand. It is is banned in both public buildings and transport, as well as outdoor areas.
    • New Zealand has a strong drinking culture and the legal drinking age is 18.

    Attire

    • In business functions, suits and ties are typically not worn - not even at expensive restaurants. For restaurants, only semi-formal clothing is required.

    Comments

    Fiji

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Similar to Australians, Fijians are also straightforward and direct when it comes to business.
    • Business meetings typically do not start on time.

    Greetings

    • In Fiji, people greet everyone who passes by with the word “Bula!” If you need help, friendly natives are readily available.

    Social Conditions

    • Smoking is culturally acceptable in public places but not in public transport.

    Attire

    • If you are traveling through local towns, dress more modestly. In popular cities, shorts, sleeveless tops and short skirts are acceptable, but could attract unwanted attention from males.
    • Most restaurants and resorts don’t require formal wear. Casual wear is much more appropriate. More upscale restaurants may require formal wear such as skirts and dresses for women.

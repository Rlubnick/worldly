    Business

    • Businesswomen commonly wear elegant business suits, dresses covering the knee and arm, or pantsuits, avoiding expensive jewelry.
    • If business cards are traded have one side translated into French or Arabic.
    • Appointments for meetings should be made as early as possible and should be confirmed a day or two before, do not schedule meetings on Friday between 11:15 a.m. and 3 p.m. since most companies will be closed for prayers.

    Greetings

    Social Conditions

    • Honour and dignity is an important part of Moroccan society and is determined by external views. One’s honour reflects down onto their extended family.
    • When one performs actions that are forbidden by society or Islam, what their doing is “Hshuma”. Hshuma is used to describe anything that brings embarrassment to the individual and family members.

    Attire

    • Conservative dressing for women is expected especially in muslim moroccan rural areas. In urban areas, clothing choice is more flexible and woman are allowed to wear more revealing pieces of clothes such as tank tops and short skirts.

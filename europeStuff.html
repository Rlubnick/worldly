    France

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Foreign women are generally accepted in business, though they may be flirted with on occasion.
    • There has been an increase of French women in management positions in retail, service, law, finance and human resources, especially in major cities.
    • Business women are allowed to invite Frenchmen to lunch or dinner. To pay the bill is not seen as a insult to frenchmen and upon arrival men initiate handshakes with women.
    • Businesswomen should wear conservative suits, dresses, pant suits, etc.

    Greetings

    • The French address all adult women, married or single, over 18 years of age with the term “Madame.”(except for waitresses, addressed as Mademoiselle.)

    Social Conditions

    • Because the French hold their privacy at a highly, Dtry to avoid asking personal questions about age, salary, occupation, children or family unless you are close.

    Attire

    • Because the French are considered leaders in fashion, dress is often times conservative and understated.
    • Casual attire is however acceptable in cities, as long as you are clean and well-dressed at all times.

    Germany

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Punctuality is extremely important to Germans, thus tardiness is sometimes seen as rude unless it is called ahead.
    • There is a common rigid vertical hierarchy where those in lower positions rarely contradict or criticize the boss publicly.
    • Within business settings, women typically wear dresses, suits, pantsuits, skirts and blouses.
    • Inviting a German man to a business dinner and paying the bill is acceptable.

    Greetings

    • It is considered rude to shake one’s hand with your other hand in a pocket.
    • Introductions commonly include your last name only. The first name basis typically comes once there is a relationship established.
    • Germans hold titles highly thus try not to use titles incorrectly or fail to use them.

    France

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Foreign women are generally accepted in business, although they may be flirted with on occasion.
    • There has been an increase of French women in management positions in retail, service, law, finance and human resources, especially in major cities.
    • Businesswomen are allowed to invite French men to lunch or dinner. To pay the bill is not seen as a insult to French men, and upon arrival, men initiate handshakes with women.
    • Businesswomen should wear conservative suits, dresses, pant suits, etc.

    Greetings

    • The French address all adult women, married or single, over 18 years of age with the term “Madame.”(except for waitresses, addressed as Mademoiselle.)

    Social Conditions

    • Because the French respect privacy, try to avoid asking personal questions about age, salary, occupation, children or family unless you are close.

    Attire

    • The French are considered to be leaders in fashion, and dress is often conservative and understated.
    • Casual attire is acceptable in cities, as long as the person is clean and well-dressed at all times.

    Comments

    England

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Conducting business for foreign women in Great Britain should run smoothly. In a meal setting, women should pay for the meal to establish a presence.
    • If a foreign woman wants to invite an Englishman to dinner, it should reconsidered to invite him for lunch instead.

    Greetings

    • Terms such as love, dearie, or darling are common English terms. Foreign women should not be offended when called those terms.

    Social Conditions

    • It is more proper to cross your legs at the ankles rather than at the knees.
    • Be aware that there is strong male-dominated presence within The 'Old Boy Network' in England, as well as throughout the U.K. However, the number in managerial positions for women have increased, compared to other European countries.

    Attire

    • Men and women wear wool and tweeds for casual occasions. Slacks, sweaters and jackets are appropriate for men and women.
    • Women usually wear suits, dresses or skirts and blouses, but in more social events, suits and cocktail dresses are more appropriate.

    Comments

    Italy

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Business attire for women and men requires conservative dress, primarily dresses and suits for women.
    • It is important to be punctual. Tardiness is seen as being sloppy, unless called ahead of time.
    • Try to avoid calling Italian business people at home, unless it is an emergency.

    Greetings

    • It is important to shake hands with everyone present when being introduced during a social or business meeting, and once more before leaving.
    • Women typically use their maiden names in business settings or legal documents. However, outside of a business setting, women typically use their married names or a combination of both.

    Social Conditions

    • Italians are often tolerant, open, and curious to the manners of others.
    • Italians enjoy good humor than can oftentimes be self-deprecating.

    Attire

    • Because Italy is a leader in fashion, even those in small towns invest a lot in fashion and their appearance.
    • Try to dress conservatively but elegantly at the same time.

    Comments

    Germany

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Punctuality is extremely important to Germans, thus tardiness is sometimes seen as rude unless it is called ahead.
    • There is a common rigid vertical hierarchy where those in lower positions rarely contradict or criticize the boss publicly.
    • Within business settings, women typically wear dresses, suits, pantsuits, skirts and blouses.
    • Inviting a German man to a business dinner and paying the bill is acceptable.

    Greetings

    • It is considered rude to shake one’s hand with your other hand in a pocket.
    • Introductions commonly include your last name only. The first name basis typically comes once there is a relationship established.
    • Germans hold titles highly. Try not to use titles incorrectly or fail to use them.

    Social Conditions

    • Germans are typically more punctual and formal than most countries.
    • It is common for men or younger people to always walk to the left of a woman.

    Attire

    • It is frowned upon to dress sloppily or with no effort.
    • It is important to be well and appropriately dressed.

    Comments

    Greece

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Although punctuality may not seem as important in Greek culture, it is important for foreigners to be on time, even if their Greek counterparts aren’t.
    • Foreign women are often able to do good business in Greece.
    • Women’s business opportunities often depend on their connections.

    Greetings

    • It is important to shake hands with everyone in the room when in a business or social meeting, and again before leaving.
    • It is common for good friends to kiss and/or embrace each other.

    Social Conditions

    • It could be problematic for a foreign woman to invite a Greek man to lunch or dinner. It is better to invite friends along or ask his wife to join for dinner.
    • Expect Greeks to ask more personal questions regarding marriage, children, etc. Try not to view it as rude, but rather as a means to get to know your more personally.

    Attire

    • Women typically wear dresses.
    • Greek dress is generally more informal than that of other European countries.

    Peru

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Business and social events usually start an hour and a half a later due to the Peruvians relaxed attitude towards time.
    • Lunch time is an important way to entertain contacts from your company.
    • Business trips that may extend several days should include an invitation for drinks or dinner at the house of the colleague

    Greetings

    • Women greet both other women and men with a kiss on the right cheek.
    • Some appropriate greetings include Buenos Días, Buenas Tardes,or Buenas Noches. To say goodbye to someone use phrases such as Chau or Bye.

    Social Conditions

    Attire

    • Revealing clothing such as low-rise pants and midriff shirts are not acceptable.
    • For female travelers in Peru, traveling clothing such as fleece wear,hiking boots and cargo are acceptable as long as the type of clothing can cover you from head to toe.

    Comments

    Brazil

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Although it is uncommon, some Brazilian people are still conservative about women working in high positions.

    Greetings

    • Women often greet each other with kisses in the cheeks. Two for married women and a third one for single women.
    • Kissing, hugging and squeezing is a cultural normality that may be uncomfortable for foreign women that value their personal face. As a traveler in Brazil you will be expected to kiss and hug both women and men you don’t know.

    Social Conditions

    • Catcalling is a common occurrence in Brazil, therefore don’t be alarmed.Most people ignore the comments, however, some become irate.
    • Don’t use the okay symbol has a sign of agreement it is considered rude, instead use thumbs up.

    Attire

    • It is common to dress elegantly, often with showy clothing, especially in business, formal and casual venues
    • If a foreign woman wants to blend in, she should avoid wearing formal, conservative clothing

    Comments

    Chile

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Dresses and suits are usual female, business attire.

    Greetings

    • A hug, kiss, and pat on the back are common occurrence in Chile especially among family members and close friends.
    • People of both genders are expected to greet everyone individually in a group with proper titles.

    Social Conditions

    • Traditionally,Chile has a male dominated society men. However, women now make up 30% of the labor and hold important political and business positions as ministers and top executives.
    • Men almost always pay the bill in a restaurant and may be embarrassed if a woman attempts to pay.

    Attire

    • Exposed legs are appropriate when wearing a dress. In addition, chilean women neither dress or favor overtly scandalous/exposing attire.

    Comments

    Costa Rica

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Business cards,proposals as well as any other official material should be printed in both English and Spanish.
    • Both women and men are expected to dress formally, however the dress is a lot more relaxed than business-wear in America.

    Greetings

    • CIt is common to say hello and goodbye to friends and acquaintances by way of a light kiss on the check- women kiss other women and men on the cheek.

    Social Conditions

    • Costa Rica is a historically male dominated culture.Traditionally, women were expected to remain house bound and dress nicely with makeup.
    • In modern day Costa Rica, sexism and gender inequality are no longer acceptable.
    • Oftentimes, Costa Rican men will make comments towards female.

    Attire

    • Despite the diverse set of women that live in Costa Rica, women of all sizes wear tight and revealing clothes .
    • Casual wear in Costa Rica is much fancier when compared to the relative idea of casual wear. Women may wear jeans often times accompained with stiletto heels and heavy makeup.

    Comments

    Argentina

    info | comments

    Information

    Business

    • Personal relationships should be made before business is done, some small talk is common before business talk.
    • Negotiations are normally do not come easily; there will most likely be multiple meetings to settle a deal.
    • Contracts are lengthy and detailed, everything will be in writing.
    • Meetings should be confirmed a week in advance

    Greetings

    • Handshakes are common when first meeting someone.
    • Titles are extremely important, especially in the presence of an elder. (Ex.Teacher="Professor", One with a PhD="Dr.", Lawyers="Abogado")
    • Greeting with a hug or kiss is considered a compliment to a woman

    Social Conditions

    • Flirtatious commentary and “catcalling” is very common. Men may call out to you while walking down the street.

    Attire

    • It is common to dress up a little more, even for casual events.
    • Women dress in more feminine clothing and will typically not wear sneakers or athletic wear in general.
